    Help, trust, and sharing

    Here’s how the math works, what stays private, and why two “winners” can both be real.

    This thing is intentionally boring in the best way: plain browser math, no login, no backend, and no mysterious “smart shopping” box pretending a dozen is a liquid.

    How to use it in 30 seconds

    1. Enter the sticker price, size, unit, and optional pack count for each option.
    2. Add any coupon or discount so the checkout math reflects reality instead of shelf-tag fan fiction.
    3. Use target amount if you need a real quantity and want the cheapest total, not just the prettiest unit price.

    Why people can trust the result

    • Mixed measurement families are excluded instead of being fake-converted into nonsense.
    • Coupons come off before unit math, so “sale” actually means sale.
    • Optional shelf-tag checks compare claimed aisle math against the price, pack count, and coupon you entered.
    • Target mode rounds up to whole packages because stores, tragically, refuse to sell 0.43 of a cereal box.
    • Your last comparison stays in this browser unless you deliberately copy a share link.

    FAQ

    Does it send my prices anywhere?

    No. The app is just static files. There is no account, no database, and no server-side calculator quietly collecting your grocery aisle crimes.

    Why can the unit-price winner and checkout winner be different?

    The best value per ounce is not always the cheapest total at the register. If the biggest pack makes you overshoot your target, a worse unit price can still win the real trip total.

    Why did one item get excluded?

    Only items in the same measurement family rank together. Weight, volume, and count stay separate on purpose so the math does not freestyle into cursed soup.

    What if the shelf tag’s unit price looks wrong?

    Enter the shelf tag’s claimed unit price and unit. The card will show whether it looks normal within rounding or noticeably off versus the price, size, pack count, and coupon you entered here.
